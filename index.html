Roasted Butternut Squash and Sweet Potato Soup Goes Over the Top Recipe
By Chef Laura Briscoe
Prep time: 15 minutes Cook time: 40 minutes Servings: Recipe Yields 36 oz (just over a quart)

Ingredients

- 1 small butternut squash, medium dice (about 2 1/2 cups)

- 1 garnet sweet potato, peeled, medium dice

- 1 small Yukon gold potato, peeled, medium dice

- 1/2 of one large sweet yellow onion, small dice

- 16 oz (1 pint) vegetable stock

- Kosher salt, to taste

- Sriracha, to taste

- 1 T per serving of Over the Top, preferred blend

- Extra Virgin Olive oil, as needed for cooking and roasting

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees convection. Toss butternut squash with a little olive oil and. Kosher salt and place on an oiled sheet tray. Repeat with the sweet potato and Yukon gold potato
  2. Place into preheated oven and roast for 15 minutes. Remove and set aside
  3. While squash is roasting, heat a medium saucepan with a little olive oil and the garlic. When garlic is aromatic, add the onion, lower the heat slightly and cook until translucent. If done before squash, turn off the heat
  4. Add squash and potato combo to the onion/garlic mixture over medium heat. Add the vegetable stock, bring to a high simmer, then cover and cook an additional 10 minutes
  5. Remove lid and turn off heat. Using either an immersion blender or countertop blender, puree soup. Note, if using a standard blender, puree in batches to manage the steam factor
  6. Taste and adjust seasonings as desired
  7. To serve, ladle into a bowl and top with Over the Top

Notes
Can be made ahead, cooled and frozen until ready to enjoy, up to three months.
